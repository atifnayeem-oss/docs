---
title: "Let merchants connect their Square"
description: "Embed one button so each merchant connects their own Square account to Ringup. No card data, no processor credentials on your side."
---

Each merchant connects their **own** Square account to Ringup with one click, from a button you drop on your dashboard. Ringup then settles on that merchant's account. You never touch card data or processor credentials.

## The mental model

Connecting a processor is the **merchant's** step, not yours, and it happens whenever the merchant is ready, asynchronously to your launch. Your job is to give them the button. When a merchant clicks it, they approve on Square's own screen; Ringup exchanges the authorization for their token **on the server** and stores it. From then on, calls that resolve to that merchant can charge on their Square account.

<Note>You never see the merchant's Square password, and the OAuth client secret never leaves Ringup's server. It is not in the button or on the page.</Note>

## Embed the button

Drop this one line wherever your merchants manage their account. It renders a styled "Connect your Square" button and starts the hosted flow.

```html
<script src="https://ringup.dev/connect/button.js"
        data-ringup-vendor="YOUR_PUBLIC_KEY"></script>
```

Optional attributes: `data-ringup-label` (button text), `data-ringup-return` (URL to send the merchant back to after connecting).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the snippet">
    Place it on your merchant-facing dashboard, with your public key in `data-ringup-vendor`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The merchant clicks Connect">
    They are taken to Square's authorization screen, approve, and are returned to a confirmation page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You get a webhook">
    Ringup fires `merchant.connected` (carrying `merchant_id`) when the connection completes, so you can flip that merchant to live in your own UI.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What the merchant grants

The button requests only what Ringup needs to recognize callers and settle on the merchant's account:

<ParamField path="MERCHANT_PROFILE_READ">Read the business profile (name, locations).</ParamField>
<ParamField path="PAYMENTS_WRITE">Take payments on the merchant's account.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="PAYMENTS_WRITE_SHARED_ONFILE">Charge a caller's saved card across the merchants on your app (the recognition primitive).</ParamField>
<ParamField path="ORDERS_WRITE">Attach payment to the order.</ParamField>
<ParamField path="CUSTOMERS_READ, CUSTOMERS_WRITE">Recognize the caller and keep their card on file.</ParamField>

## Go-live checklist

<Check>Embed the button on your dashboard with your public key.</Check>
<Check>A merchant connects their Square account (their step, any time).</Check>
<Check>You receive `merchant.connected` and mark them live.</Check>

Until a merchant connects, the payment layer stays inaudible on their calls (`create_checkout` returns `payment_required: "none"`), so you can ship Ringup across your whole fleet while merchants onboard at their own pace.
