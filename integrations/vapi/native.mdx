Vapi supports two ways to add Ringup payments. **Native handoff** (this page) keeps the caller
in your agent's own voice from greeting to receipt and is the recommended path on Vapi. The
other is [call transfer](/integrations/vapi/call-transfer), for standardizing one integration
across several platforms.

Returning callers are recognized by phone number and pay with their saved card in one sentence.
You write no payment prompt: the payment step inherits your agent's voice, model, and
transcriber.

## Prerequisites

- A Vapi agent (or squad) you already run
- Your Vapi API key (private key)
- A Ringup account. Test mode works out of the box on a shared sandbox, so there is nothing
  to connect. See [Testing](/concepts/testing).

## Set up

Both paths build the same thing, and both leave everything you own untouched until the final
go-live step: your assistant stays byte-for-byte unmodified, and rollback restores it exactly.
The dashboard registers every object for you; the API path is the same wiring, done by hand in
your own Vapi account.

<Tabs>
<Tab title="Using the dashboard">

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect Vapi, once for your account">
    In the Ringup dashboard, open **Integrations**, then **Connect Vapi**. Paste your
    Vapi API key (private key). Ringup uses it to list your agents and provision the payment
    objects, and never stores it. One connection covers every merchant you run.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the agents that take payments">
    Select the agent that answers merchant calls (or the squad plus the member that confirms
    the order). Ringup assembles the payment step, the wrap-up step, the `ringup_*` tools, and
    the handoff tool around it, then shows the one prompt line to paste. There is no
    per-merchant step: the merchant resolves from the dialed number on every call, and each
    merchant activates when it connects its processor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your webhook endpoint">
    Under **Settings, then Webhooks**, add your HTTPS endpoint and copy the signing secret.
    Ringup sends a signed `checkout.completed` there for every payment, so your server can
    mark orders paid. See [Webhooks](/concepts/webhooks).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rehearse on a private line">
    The dashboard binds a test twin of your agent, with the two go-live changes baked in, to
    a rehearsal number only you know about. Call it from a real phone, order, and pay with the
    test card: you hear the silent handoff, the recognition, the charge, and the wrap-up with
    a confirmation code. See [Vapi testing](/integrations/vapi/testing).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go live">
    When the rehearsal call reads a confirmation code back, click **Go live**. Ringup points
    your phone number at the squad first, then makes exactly two changes to your assistant:
    it appends the handoff tool to its toolIds and appends the one prompt line. Running
    several numbers or locations? Move one number first as a canary. **Roll back** any time:
    it removes exactly those two changes and points each number back where it was, restoring
    your assistant byte-for-byte.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Tab>
<Tab title="Using the API">

Own every object in your own Vapi account. You create the same pieces the dashboard
registers, assemble the squad, rehearse, and flip your number when ready.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the five ringup_* tools">
    Register `create_checkout`, `identify`, `charge`, `payment_status`, and `send_link` as
    static tools against Ringup's API. Caller and merchant resolve server-side from trusted
    headers, never from model-typed arguments. Call `identify` at the greeting, not at
    payment, so a returning caller is greeted by name
    ([Recognize at the start of the call](/concepts/identity)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the payment step">
    An assistant that silently checks whether payment is needed for THIS purchase, then
    takes it, offers it, or says nothing at all. It inherits your agent's voice, model, and
    transcriber, and carries the five tools from the previous step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the wrap-up step">
    Your own agent in wrap-up mode: its prompt verbatim with only the task replaced. It
    announces success, reads back the payment confirmation number, thanks the caller, and
    ends the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assemble the squad">
    Create a new squad whose `members` array references your agent live (a plain reference,
    not a copy), then the payment step, then the wrap-up step. Never hand back to your
    greeter after payment: the payment step exits to wrap-up on its own, and the wrap-up
    ends the call. Your original agent and squad keep running untouched; the new squad
    carries no traffic until a number points at it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the handoff tool">
    A saved handoff tool (`handoff_to_Payment`) on the member that confirms the order. Note:
    member `assistantOverrides` cannot deliver tools on voice calls (toolIds are ignored;
    inline tools reach chat only); the saved assistant is the only wiring that works on
    voice. You attach it at go-live, not yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your webhook endpoint">
    In the Ringup dashboard, under **Settings, then Webhooks**, add your HTTPS endpoint and
    copy the signing secret. Your server verifies the signature and handles the
    `checkout.completed` event. See [Webhooks](/concepts/webhooks).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rehearse on a private line">
    Rehearse before anything you own changes. Make a byte-copy twin of your order-confirming
    assistant with the two go-live changes baked in (the handoff tool appended to its
    toolIds, the one prompt line appended). Build a rehearsal squad, twin then payment step
    then wrap-up step, and point a spare test number at it. Call it from a real phone: you
    hear the silent handoff, the recognition, the policy branch, the charge, and the wrap-up
    with a confirmation code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go live">
    Record where each number points, then make two moves in this order:

    1. **Point your phone number at the squad.** Number first on purpose: a caller landing
       in the seconds mid-switch simply gets your current payment-free flow. (The reverse
       order strands callers with an agent reaching for a payment step that is not there
       yet.)
    2. **Make exactly two changes to your assistant**: append the handoff tool to its
       `model.toolIds`, and append one line to its prompt (hand off once the order and name
       are confirmed). Keep your existing toolIds: a PATCH replaces the whole `model`
       object.

    Running several numbers or locations? Move one number first as a canary. Keep the
    rehearsal rig if you want a permanent test line.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Roll back (any time)">
    Reverse exactly the go-live moves: remove the handoff tool from `model.toolIds`, remove
    the prompt line, and point each number back at its recorded pre-go-live destination.
    Your assistant is restored byte-for-byte.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
Vapi's dashboard does not render squad-scoped wiring or apiRequest tool names; the squad
canvas may show "No handoff tools configured" for working wiring. Read the squad and assistant
back from the Vapi API to verify: it is the source of truth. See
[Vapi testing](/integrations/vapi/testing) for what to check.
</Note>

You are never asked for a business name, a payment policy, a model, a transcriber, or a
language. The business is identified from the number the call comes in on; the policy is a
merchant setting served by Ringup per purchase; everything else is inherited from your agent.

## The call flow, on every call, for every merchant

```text
your agent takes the order -> "One moment." (always the same silent handoff)
  -> payment step creates a Checkout Session; Ringup returns payment_required for THIS purchase:
       required  -> "Let's knock out payment now..." (must complete before the call ends)
       optional  -> one offer, never blocks ("Want to take care of payment now?")
       none      -> says nothing at all
  -> wrap-up (your agent): announces success, reads the confirmation number, closes politely
```

Your agent never branches on payment logic, and a merchant changing their payment rule (or
connecting later) changes call behavior immediately with no agent edits.

## Test it

Run one full payment in test mode before go-live. Call the rehearsal number from a real phone,
order, and confirm: the recognized test caller (`+14155550142`) pays on the saved Visa ending
5858 in one sentence; from any other phone you are texted a pay link and enter the sandbox card
`4111 1111 1111 1111`. Expect the wrap-up step to read a confirmation code back and a test-mode
`checkout.completed` at your endpoint. Fixtures and the decline card are in
[Testing](/concepts/testing); Vapi specifics (the rehearsal twin, verifying wiring through the
Vapi API) are in [Vapi testing](/integrations/vapi/testing).

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Do I write a payment prompt?">
    No. The payment step ships pre-validated and inherits your agent's voice and model. You
    change nothing about how it works.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How does Ringup know the caller and the business?">
    Server-side: the caller number and the dialed number arrive on trusted headers set by
    the platform, never typed by the model, so neither can be spoofed or hallucinated. See
    [Caller Identity](/concepts/identity) and [Merchant Identity](/concepts/merchant-identity).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if Ringup is unreachable mid-call?">
    The handoff fails neutrally ("Give me one second.") and your agent continues. The
    scripted line never asserts an outcome, so a caller is never told payment happened (or
    was not needed) when Ringup could not be reached.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What does a merchant have to do?">
    Connect their payment processor to Ringup once. Until they do, their calls behave
    exactly as they do today; the moment they connect, payment starts happening on their
    calls with no agent changes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
