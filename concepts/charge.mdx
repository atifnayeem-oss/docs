title: "Charge"
description: "Charge the card the caller picked, against an open Checkout Session, off-session on the merchant's own processor. A decline is a 402, not a result."
---

`charge` runs one saved card against one open [Checkout Session](/concepts/checkout-session). The
caller is already recognized and the session already resolved the amount, so a charge takes two
values: which session, and which card the caller agreed to. Ringup charges it
<Tooltip tip="A charge run against a card the caller saved earlier, with their consent, while they are not actively entering card details.">off-session</Tooltip>
and settles the money on the merchant's own processor. Because the caller never reads a card aloud,
no card data touches your agent.

## The card is never defaulted

`instrument_id` is required. Ringup will not pick a card for you, even when the caller has exactly
one, and even though the session hands you `default_card_id`.

The reason is the conversation. The caller agreed out loud to a **specific** card, and a voice agent
mishears. "Use my other one" that gets dropped, and an implicit default, is how the wrong card gets
charged with a spoken yes on the record for a different one. Naming the card in the request makes
your agent's choice explicit and auditable.

## The three statuses

| `status` | What it means |
|---|---|
| `completed` | The money moved. This is the only status you announce. |
| `pending` | Not settled. The processor has not confirmed an outcome. |
| `failed` | Nothing was charged. |

`pending` is the one that needs care, because it does **not** mean "nothing happened". It means the
outcome is unknown: the payment may already have gone through. That is why the safe response is to
wait rather than to charge again, and why your agent should not tell the caller the payment failed.

Do not start a second payment for the same order. If you want to retry, repeat the **same**
`charge` call: it carries the same idempotency key, so it cannot charge twice. Otherwise read the
outcome from the [webhook](/concepts/webhooks) or by
[reading the session back](/concepts/checkout-session). We resolve a pending charge for you by
asking the processor what really happened.

Never tell the caller payment happened without a `completed` status.

## A decline is a 402

A declined card is an error, not a result. It comes back as `402` in the standard
[error envelope](/api-reference/errors), carrying the decline code and a sentence your agent can
speak, so an ordinary `if (!response.ok)` check catches it. It never arrives as a `200` with a sad
status inside.

## The confirmation number is the order id

`order_id` is what the agent reads back. It is the **merchant's** own number, so the caller can
quote it to the merchant and the merchant will find it. A Ringup id would be a number only Ringup
can look up, which is no use to a caller standing at a counter.

## Idempotent under retries

`replay` is `true` when this exact charge already happened and you are looking at the original. A
platform that auto-retries a slow tool call, a model that fires twice, a network retry: all of them
return the first charge instead of taking the money again. This is a server guarantee, not a client
convention.
