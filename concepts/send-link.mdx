When a [Checkout Session](/concepts/checkout-session) comes back with an empty `cards` array, there
is nothing to charge yet. `send_link` texts the caller a secure pay link so they pay on their own
phone, without ever reading a card number aloud. It is the first-time path in both integration
modes, and once they pay they are [recognized](/concepts/identity) on every call after, at any
Ringup merchant.

## The whole path

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a session when the order is ready">
    [`create_checkout`](/concepts/checkout-session) resolves the amount and returns `cards`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find no card to offer">
    `cards` is empty, so there is nothing the caller can approve on the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask, then text the link">
    Send the link only after the caller agrees to the text.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The session is the only input

`send_link` takes one field: `checkout_session_id`. The caller, the merchant, the amount and the
policy all live on the session already, so passing them again could only introduce a disagreement.
This is what makes a pay link trustworthy: it can never carry a total nobody derived from a real
order, and it can never be texted to a number the session was not opened for.

## Check `sent` before you speak

`sent` is `false` when a repeat send inside a short dedupe window returned the same link **without
texting again**. A duplicate is common: platforms retry a slow tool call, and models fire twice.

Read the field. An agent that assumes a `200` means a text went out tells the caller "I have texted
you" when nothing went out, and the caller waits for a message that will never arrive. On `false`,
say the link was already sent and point them at the earlier message.

A caller whose link genuinely never arrived and who asks again seconds later is outside the window
and gets a fresh text, so resending always works.

## During the call or after

Payment can complete either way:

- **During the call.** The caller pays while still on the phone. Poll the
  [session](/concepts/checkout-session) so your agent knows the moment it clears and can close the
  call knowing the order is paid.
- **After the call ends.** The caller hangs up and pays later from the same link. The call does not
  have to wait.

Either way the caller gets an SMS receipt, and your server learns the outcome from a
`checkout.succeeded` [webhook](/concepts/webhooks). A link payment is not a separate event, so one
webhook handler covers both a saved-card charge and a first-time link payment.

Once the caller has paid once, their card shows up in `cards` on the next session and you can
[`charge`](/concepts/charge) it directly.
