The **Checkout Session** is the object every payment turns on. You create one the moment an order is
ready to pay, and it carries everything the payment step needs: the resolved amount, Ringup's
[policy decision](/concepts/payment-policy) for this purchase, the caller's cards that are
chargeable at this merchant, and, when payment runs by transfer, where to send the live call.

Both integration modes start here. Whether your agent charges a card itself or hands the call to
Ringup's payment line, the session is the same object.

## What you send

| Field | Required | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| `merchant_id` | yes | Which business the caller reached. Sets the policy, the processor, and which cards are chargeable. |
| `caller_phone` | yes | The caller, E.164. The same number [`identify`](/concepts/identity) took. |
| `order_id` | no | The merchant's own order. The amount authority when present. |
| `amount_cents` | only without `order_id` | The total in cents. |
| `currency` | no | Defaults to `USD`. |
| `return_to` | no | Where to send the caller back after payment. |
| `platform_call_id` | no | Your platform's call id. Recommended: it ties the payment to your call logs. |
| `success_message` | no | Spoken after a successful payment. |
| `failure_message` | no | Spoken after a failed one. |
| `metadata` | no | Your own key/value pairs. Stored, echoed back, never interpreted by Ringup. |

## The order is the amount authority

When you pass `order_id`, Ringup reads the total from the merchant's own order and returns it as
`amount_cents` with `amount_source: "order"`. You do not have to compute the total, and a total
nobody derived from a real order cannot reach the caller.

`amount_cents` is required only when there is no `order_id`, and it is never trusted over an order.
If you send both and they disagree, the request fails with `409 amount_mismatch` rather than
quietly charging one of the two. The response says where the number came from:

- `amount_source: "order"`: resolved from the merchant's order.
- `amount_source: "request"`: the amount you sent, because there was no order to read.

## The cards are the ones chargeable here

`cards` holds the caller's saved cards that can actually be charged **at this merchant**, newest
first. Each entry is the same shape everywhere a card appears:

```json
{ "instrument_id": "ins_4b7e", "brand": "VISA", "last_four": "5858", "expiry": "07/2028" }
```

Offer them by brand and last four, then pass the chosen `instrument_id` to
[`charge`](/concepts/charge).

This filter is handle-aware, not merely processor-aware. On a processor whose card-on-file is
scoped per merchant, a card the caller enrolled at one merchant has no charge handle at another,
and it cannot get one without the card again. Listing it would read fine to your agent and then
fail at the charge, after the caller had already said yes to it. So a card that cannot be charged
here is not offered here.

An empty `cards` array means there is nothing to offer: [text a link](/concepts/send-link) instead.

`default_card_id` is the newest card, a suggestion for what to offer first. It is never charged
implicitly: `charge` always requires an explicit `instrument_id`.

## Two ways it is used

A Checkout Session works two ways, and the difference is invisible to your order logic.

- **In your agent (API).** Your agent reads the session, offers a card from `cards`, and calls
  [`charge`](/concepts/charge) itself. Nothing transfers. The flexible path.
- **Call transfer.** If you have ever used a hosted web checkout, you already know this shape. In a
  web checkout you create a session, redirect the browser to a hosted page, and pass a return URL;
  the customer pays and lands back on your site. A Ringup call transfer is the same over the phone:
  `transfer_to` is the checkout URL, transferring the call is the redirect, and `return_to` is the
  return URL. The caller pays on the Ringup line and the call comes back to your agent.

`transfer_to` (in the response) is where you send the call. `return_to` (in the request) is where
Ringup sends the caller back. They are the two ends of the same trip. `transfer_to` is `null`
whenever payment is not required, because there is nothing to transfer to.

## Three ways a transfer ends

However the transfer ends, the payment itself is identical: Ringup charges the caller, texts them an
SMS receipt, and posts a [webhook](/concepts/webhooks) to your server. The three options below
change only what the caller hears and where the call goes next, never whether they get a receipt or
whether you get the webhook.

`return_to` and the spoken messages are complementary. Use whichever fits; you do not need both.

1. **Transfer back (`return_to`).** Ringup returns the caller to your agent and attaches the outcome
   as SIP headers, so your agent resumes already knowing payment succeeded and closes in its own
   voice. Best when you want to keep talking (upsell, fulfillment, goodbye).
2. **Ringup speaks the result (`success_message`).** No transfer back: Ringup reads your line and
   ends the call. Best when payment is the last thing on the call but you want the closing words in
   your words.
3. **Neither.** Ringup reads a short default confirmation and ends.

A `return_to` that cannot connect falls back to speaking `success_message` (or the default) and
ending, so a failed return never strands the caller.

## Read a session back

To check on a session later, fetch it by id:

```bash
curl https://api.ringup.dev/v1/checkout_sessions/cs_x9f2 \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer <YOUR_RINGUP_KEY>"
```

```json Response
{
  "session_status": "complete",
  "payment_required": "required",
  "amount_cents": 1900,
  "currency": "USD",
  "return_to": "sip:agent@your-number",
  "charge": {
    "charge_id": "chg_5m1p",
    "status": "completed",
    "amount_cents": 1900,
    "processor_payment_id": "pi_3Qx8"
  },
  "checkout_session_id": "cs_x9f2",
  "merchant_id": "tonys-pizza",
  "order_id": "ord_7f21",
  "platform_call_id": "call_abc123",
  "metadata": null
}
```

`session_status` is `open`, `complete`, or `expired`, and it describes the session. The charge has
its own `status` inside `charge`, which is `null` until something is attempted. This is a read, so a
declined charge reads as `200` with a failed charge rather than an error.

## Reconcile from the webhook

However the call ends, your server learns the outcome from a `checkout.completed`
[webhook](/concepts/webhooks). That is your source of truth, independent of what the caller heard,
and it is what you flip your own order on.
