---
title: "Payment policy"
description: "Whether payment is required, optional, or absent is a merchant setting Ringup serves per purchase, returned as payment_required on the Checkout Session."
---

Whether an order needs payment is not logic in your agent. It is a merchant setting Ringup
resolves per purchase and returns as the `payment_required` field when you create a Checkout
Session. The merchant changes the rule with no redeploy, and your agent never hard-codes it.

## The three values

When you create a Checkout Session, `payment_required` comes back as one of:

| Value | Meaning | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| `required` | Payment must complete before the call ends | The session returns a `transfer_to`; take payment |
| `optional` | Offer once, never block; unpaid is a valid outcome | The session returns a `transfer_to`; offer, but do not require |
| `none` | No payment for this business or purchase | No `transfer_to`; carry on and end the call normally |

## It is per purchase, not per merchant

The same merchant can produce different values on different orders. A parts shop might make
payment optional under $50 and required above it; only the amount changes the rule. That is why
creating the session passes `amount_cents`, and why the answer cannot be cached across calls.

## `none` is the graceful floor

Unknown merchant, unconnected processor, a business that simply does not take phone payments:
all of these resolve to `none`, and the payment layer becomes inaudible. This is a design
guarantee, not an error path. It means the same agent configuration is safe to run in front of
every merchant on your platform, connected or not, from day one.

<Note>
Ringup's payment step (or your agent, depending on the integration) reads `payment_required` and
branches on it, so your agent never hard-codes payment logic. In a call transfer you read it off
the Checkout Session directly.
</Note>
