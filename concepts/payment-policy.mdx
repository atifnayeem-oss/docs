---
title: "Payment policy"
description: "Whether payment is required, optional, or absent is a merchant setting Ringup serves per purchase, returned as payment_required on the Checkout Session."
---

Whether an order needs payment is not logic in your agent. It is a merchant setting Ringup
resolves per purchase and returns as the `payment_required` field when you create a Checkout
Session. The merchant changes the rule with no redeploy, and your agent never hard-codes it.

## The three values

When you create a Checkout Session, `payment_required` comes back as one of:

| Value | Meaning | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| `required` | Payment must complete before the call ends | Take payment: charge the saved card, or in hosted mode transfer to the payment line |
| `optional` | Offer once, never block; unpaid is a valid outcome | Offer payment, but do not require it |
| `none` | No payment for this business or purchase | Carry on and end the call normally |

## It is per purchase, not per merchant

The same merchant can produce different values on different orders. A parts shop might make
payment optional under $50 and required above it; only the amount changes the rule. That is why the
session resolves the amount before the verdict, and why the answer cannot be cached across calls.

## `none` is the graceful floor

`none` has two possible meanings, and you never have to tell them apart:

- **The merchant has no processor connected.** There is nothing to charge on. This is not an error:
  creating a session for an unconnected merchant returns `201` with `payment_required: "none"`, and
  `transfer_to` is `null`.
- **The merchant's policy says this purchase does not need payment.** A business that takes payment
  at pickup, or an order under a threshold that the merchant chose to let through.

The correct response to either is the same: do not transfer, do not offer a card, carry on with the
call and end it normally. That is why they share one value. Your agent branches on `payment_required`
and needs no separate "is this merchant set up yet" check, which is what makes one agent
configuration safe to run in front of every merchant on your platform, connected or not, from day
one.

<Note>
Whoever runs the payment reads `payment_required` and branches on it, so your agent never hard-codes
payment logic. In hosted mode Ringup's payment step reads it off the Checkout Session; in the
in-agent API path your agent reads it and decides whether to [`charge`](/concepts/charge). Either
way the merchant's rule, not your code, decides.
</Note>
